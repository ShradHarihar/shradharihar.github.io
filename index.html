01 — Introduction

About Me

I am Alex Morgan, an MSc graduate in Computer Science from the University of Example, specialising in the mathematical foundations of Graph Neural Networks. My research sits at the intersection of algebraic topology, spectral graph theory, and deep learning.

I am passionate about building rigorous theoretical frameworks that explain why GNNs work — not just how. My MSc thesis explored the expressive power of message-passing neural networks through the lens of the Weisfeiler–Leman graph isomorphism test.

I am currently seeking PhD opportunities in theoretical machine learning, graph representation learning, or geometric deep learning.

"My goal is to develop a unified mathematical theory for Graph Neural Networks that bridges combinatorics, topology, and learning theory — enabling principled design of more expressive and interpretable architectures."
02 — Focus Areas

Research Interests

Graph Neural Network Theory
Expressive power, universality, and limitations of GNN architectures through algebraic and combinatorial lenses.
Spectral Graph Theory
Eigenvalue analysis of graph Laplacians and their role in designing spectral convolution operators.
Geometric Deep Learning
Symmetry, equivariance, and invariance principles applied to learning on non-Euclidean data structures.
Topological Data Analysis
Persistent homology and simplicial complexes as tools for understanding data shape and GNN expressivity.
Graph Representation Learning
Learning low-dimensional embeddings of graphs and nodes for downstream reasoning tasks.
