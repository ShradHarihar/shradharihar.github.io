About Me

Hey, I am Shraddha Harihar, a mathematician who got curious about how neural networks actually work, went looking for the math behind them, and ended up in graph theory. One question kept leading to the next, and before long it stopped being curiosity and started being the work itself.

My focus is on the mathematical foundations of graph neural networks. Understanding what these networks can and cannot do, and why, from the structure of the graph up. Whether it is spectral methods, combinatorial properties, or where graph theory meets cryptography, the question I always start with is: what does the mathematics actually say?

Research Interests

Mathematical Foundations of GNNs
Understanding the math behind graph neural networks, spanning spectral graph theory, linear algebra, and how concepts from topology shape the way these networks think and behave.
Structural Graph Theory and GNNs
Exploring connectivity, domination, and combinatorial graph properties that go beyond surfaces and directly inform how graph neural networks are built and understood.
Cryptography meets Graph Neural Networks
Using GNNs to study and analyze cryptographic systems, exploring how graph structure can reveal what traditional approaches might miss.
Learning on Graphs, Beyond Theory
Bringing mathematically sound GNN methods to real problems, with geometric deep learning as a natural direction, where getting the math right is what makes the application actually trustworthy.
